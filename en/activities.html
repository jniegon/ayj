If it’s your first visit to Austin, be sure to explore a few of the city’s iconic spots. The State Capitol building is a National Historic Landmark, built from local Oak Hill limestone and and sunset red granite.

At the heart of Austin, Zilker Park offers large green spaces, trails, gardens, and many other outdoor amenities. Lady Bird Lake is a popular recreation area where you can enjoy a promenade or water activities.

The University of Texas is home to 50,000+ students. Among its 7 museums and 17 libraries, you can find one of only 21 remaining copies of the Gutenberg Bible and the first permanent photograph.

6th Street is the heart of Austin’s famous live music scene. Spanning downtown, you'll find laid back patios on West Sixth, high-energy nightlife on “Dirty Sixth”, and a mix of eclectic dive bars and cocktail bars East of I-35.

The trendy South Congress neighborhood is a blend of old Austin character and new hipster vibe. Here you'll find restaurants, music venues, galleries, and unique shops.

On Rainey Street, east of downtown, renovated houses have been turned into bungalow bars. Day or night, you’ll find relaxed bar-goers, often with their dogs in tow.

Enjoy the nice weather in Texas and take the opportunity to go for a swim in one of the natural spring water preserves around the River House.

The most popular spots are Hamilton Pool Preserve (35-minute drive), Krause Springs (20-minute drive), and Barton Springs Pool (35-minute drive, close to downtown Austin). Check their websites for more information.

Additionally, there is access from the house to the Pedernales River, although the hiking trail there might be a little hazardous.
